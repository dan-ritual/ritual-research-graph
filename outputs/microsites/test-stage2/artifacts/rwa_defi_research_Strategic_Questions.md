# Strategic Questions: RWA DeFi Research

## How should Ritual position itself in the fragmented RWA infrastructure landscape?

**Context:** The RWA space shows a clear bifurcation between compliance-first approaches (Provenance) and composability-focused solutions (Plume), with most protocols operating in silos. With $35B+ in tokenized assets but limited AI integration, Ritual faces critical positioning decisions that will determine market access and competitive advantage.

**Analysis:** Three distinct positioning strategies emerge: (1) Protocol-agnostic infrastructure layer providing AI services across all RWA platforms, (2) Deep partnership model focusing on 2-3 key protocols like Zivo and Credit Co-op, or (3) Compliance-first approach targeting institutional players through Provenance-style integrations.

The infrastructure layer approach offers maximum market reach but requires significant technical complexity to integrate with diverse protocol architectures. The deep partnership model provides faster time-to-market and clearer value demonstration but limits addressable market. The compliance-first approach aligns with institutional adoption trends but may sacrifice crypto-native innovation opportunities.

**Implications for Ritual:** Recommend a hybrid approach: establish deep partnerships with 2-3 emerging protocols (Zivo, Credit Co-op) as proof points while building protocol-agnostic infrastructure. This allows rapid market validation while maintaining scalability. Prioritize privacy-preserving capabilities that address both compliance requirements and DeFi composability needs.

## What specific AI capabilities address the most critical bottlenecks in RWA protocols?

**Context:** The research reveals three key bottlenecks: idle capital in lending protocols like Zivo, manual KYC/onboarding processes at Credit Co-op, and lack of transparent on-chain analytics across the sector. These bottlenecks directly impact capital efficiency and institutional adoption, representing clear AI intervention opportunities.

**Analysis:** Idle capital emerges as the highest-impact problem - Zivo's merchant cash advance model creates gaps between loan fulfillment cycles where capital sits unused. AI-powered loan origination could automate underwriting, match borrowers with available capital, and optimize lending parameters in real-time. This addresses a fundamental economic inefficiency affecting protocol returns.

Manual KYC processes at Credit Co-op and similar protocols create onboarding friction that limits user acquisition. AI could automate identity verification, cash flow analysis, and risk assessment while maintaining privacy through encrypted computation. This directly accelerates user growth and reduces operational costs.

The lack of transparent, on-chain analytics represents a competitive moat opportunity. Most protocols use off-chain, black-box risk models that institutions cannot audit. On-chain AI models with verifiable computation could become a key differentiator for institutional adoption.

**Implications for Ritual:** Prioritize automated loan origination as the primary use case - it has clear ROI metrics and addresses capital efficiency directly. Develop privacy-preserving KYC automation as a secondary capability that enhances the loan origination value proposition. Build transparent, auditable AI models as a competitive differentiator against existing off-chain solutions.

**Open threads:** Need to quantify idle capital percentages across different RWA lending protocols. Requires technical validation of real-time loan matching feasibility. Must assess regulatory requirements for AI-powered KYC in different jurisdictions.

## What role should privacy-preserving computation play in Ritual's RWA strategy?

**Context:** Institutional RWA adoption faces a fundamental tension between blockchain transparency and regulatory privacy requirements. Provenance's $20B TVL (albeit controversial) demonstrates institutional scale, but verification difficulties highlight the challenges of balancing transparency with privacy. This creates a unique opportunity for privacy-preserving AI solutions.

**Analysis:** Privacy-preserving computation addresses multiple critical needs: enabling institutional compliance while maintaining on-chain verification, protecting sensitive financial data while enabling AI analysis, and creating competitive moats through proprietary data insights. The regulatory environment increasingly demands privacy protection, making this capability essential rather than optional.

Current RWA protocols largely use off-chain analytics to address privacy concerns, but this sacrifices transparency and composability. Privacy-preserving AI could enable on-chain analytics while protecting sensitive data, creating the best of both worlds. This is particularly relevant for KYC processes, credit scoring, and risk assessment where data sensitivity is paramount.

The technical complexity of privacy-preserving computation could become a significant barrier to entry for competitors, creating sustainable competitive advantages. However, it also increases development costs and time-to-market, requiring careful prioritization of use cases.

**Implications for Ritual:** Make privacy-preserving computation a core differentiator rather than an optional feature. Focus on use cases where privacy is regulatory requirement rather than nice-to-have. Develop clear value propositions that quantify the benefits of on-chain privacy versus off-chain alternatives.

## How should Ritual approach the multichain RWA ecosystem?

**Context:** The RWA stack has evolved into a multichain architecture with Ethereum as the institutional settlement layer and specialized L2s like Provenance and Plume serving specific use cases. This fragmentation creates both opportunities and challenges for AI infrastructure providers seeking to serve the entire ecosystem.

**Analysis:** The multichain reality requires strategic decisions about where to deploy AI capabilities and how to maintain consistency across different chains. Ethereum's institutional legitimacy (validated by BlackRock's choice) makes it essential for credibility, but specialized L2s may offer better economics and functionality for specific use cases.

Cross-chain AI services could become a significant value proposition, enabling protocols to access sophisticated analytics regardless of their underlying blockchain. This requires technical capabilities for cross-chain data aggregation and model deployment, but could create network effects as protocols benefit from shared AI insights.

However, multichain deployment increases technical complexity and operational overhead. Different chains have varying performance characteristics, security models, and developer ecosystems that impact AI service delivery. Resource allocation across chains becomes a critical strategic decision.

**Implications for Ritual:** Develop a hub-and-spoke model with core AI infrastructure on Ethereum and specialized deployments on key RWA L2s. Prioritize Provenance and Plume based on their complementary approaches to RWA infrastructure. Create cross-chain data aggregation capabilities that enhance AI model performance through broader data access.

**Open threads:** Need technical evaluation of AI deployment requirements across different RWA chains. Requires partnership discussions with key L2 teams to understand integration opportunities. Must assess economic models for cross-chain AI services.
